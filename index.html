The Idea

Restart the 1787 experiment. Skip the accumulation.

The American founding was a minimal constitutional protocol that acquired 250 years of administrative sediment. The Príncipe Charter runs the original experiment again on clean hardware: a negative-rights constitution under which the state exists to do exactly two things - referee disputes and maintain a passive defense shield - and is constitutionally forbidden from doing anything else.

No legislature. No agencies. No income tax. No government debt. Everything else - infrastructure, medicine, money, education - is voluntary exchange between free people, protected by seven inviolable rights and a ~50-page Founding Code.

And it is built for permanence. The Charter's rights cannot be amended, its powers cannot grow without unanimous consent, and its sovereignty horizon is measured in centuries - a country designed to outlast its founders, not a project designed to exit.

The Island

Príncipe. On the equator. In the Atlantic.

Príncipe is a drowned volcano wrapped in primary rainforest - green phonolite towers rising out of the southern jungle, golden-sand bays, waterfalls that run straight into the sea. It is one of the least-visited islands on Earth and a UNESCO biosphere dense with species that exist nowhere else. The beauty is not incidental to the project; it is a founding asset. Nobody relocates a family, a company, or a fortune to an ugly place.

Illustrative schematic - not a survey map
  • Location: Gulf of Guinea, ~250 km off West Africa, part of São Tomé and Príncipe (STP)
  • Population: ~8,000 residents - each offered a voluntary Clean Break payment of ~$100,000, roughly 24 years of average income
  • Conservation: 40% of the island is Obo National Park - untouched rainforest and volcanic peaks; the Charter locks 90% of it as permanent, constitutionally irrevocable conservation land, stronger than the current UNESCO designation
  • Development zone: ~82 km² across 7 sovereign districts, each free to compete on governance above the Charter floor
  • Existing assets: working airport, working port, power, connectivity - day-one foundations
The Bill of Rights

Seven pillars. All negative. None amendable.

I

Self-Ownership

Absolute bodily and neural autonomy. No FDA equivalent - disclosure, not approval. Private certification markets.

II

Property

No eminent domain. No forfeiture. No qualifications. The state has no mechanism to take, ever.

III

Signal

Zero speech laws. Fraud, defamation, and doxxing are prosecuted as violations of the other rights - never of speech itself.

IV

Defense

Individual arms rights absolute. National defense is a passive shield: AI maritime surveillance, cyber hardening, non-kinetic denial.

V

Contract

Total sanctity of voluntary agreements. No "unconscionability" backdoor for discretionary power to grow through.

VI

Sound Money

No central bank. Gold-pegged unit of account. All currencies welcome. Government fees payable in anything, at market rate.

VII

No Debt

Government borrowing constitutionally forbidden. No exceptions, no emergency clause. Catastrophe insurance instead.

The State

Two functions. Triple-locked against growth.

The Referee

A Supreme Court of 3-5 judges imported from respected common law jurisdictions, chosen by an independent international committee - never by the capital provider. 15-year non-renewable terms, salaries ring-fenced from endowment yield. Loser-pays courts with a private contingency market, so access to justice doesn't require wealth.

The Guard

A passive defense shield: AI maritime surveillance, cyber defense, coastal area denial, catastrophe insurance. No standing army, no offensive capability. Defense reframed as resilience - because the realistic threat to a small free jurisdiction is economic coercion, not invasion.

1. Enumerated & Locked

Federal powers exhaustively listed. Adding one requires unanimous consent of all 7 districts.

2. 10-Year Sunset

Every expansion of federal jurisdiction auto-expires unless actively reaffirmed by supermajority each decade.

3. District Nullification

Any single district can nullify a federal expansion within its borders. Creep must win everywhere, forever. Liberty only has to win once.

Sovereignty

Two paths. Sequenced honestly.

STP's constitution defines the national territory as including Príncipe, and no state has sold sovereign territory in the modern era. This framework does not pretend otherwise - it sequences around it.

Phase 1 - The Special Jurisdiction

Enabling legislation, negotiated with the people who already govern Príncipe

Príncipe has had its own Autonomous Regional Government since 1995 - a real counterparty. Phase 1 is a special governance jurisdiction created by STP enabling legislation and negotiated jointly with the Regional Government: the Charter as governing framework, its own courts under the Founding Code, the 2% fiscal model - while STP retains formal sovereignty and earns an ongoing revenue share.

The precedent is Honduras's ZEDE regime and Próspera - including the lesson of the 2022 repeal. Mitigations: bilateral investment treaty protection, ICSID arbitration, land assembly exclusively through voluntary purchase, and an economic structure that makes the jurisdiction more valuable to STP alive than dead.

Phase 2 - The Free Association Compact

Full internal sovereignty for 200+ years, ratified by a nation that has already seen it work

The endgame: a Cook Islands-style Free Association compact granting the Federation full internal sovereignty - constitution, courts, immigration, money - for 200+ years, with an independence referendum pathway. In exchange, STP receives a package worth several times its entire annual GDP across the program - the compact contribution, the resident payments, and an ongoing revenue share that keeps paying long after the signing. It requires constitutional amendment and, realistically, a national referendum in STP. Which is exactly why Phase 1 comes first: referendums are won on demonstrated benefit, not projections.

What We Haven't Solved

Open questions. On purpose.

A framework that claims to have no unsolved problems is advertising that it hasn't been stress-tested. These are ours:

  • The exchange rate oracle - the exact mechanism for gold-to-currency conversion at the point of government fee payment.
  • The Founding Code itself - the ~50 pages of statutory law covering contracts, property, and torts are specified but not yet drafted.
  • The independence referendum trigger - what precisely activates the post-compact vote, and who is eligible.
  • Phase 1 jurisdiction boundary - which land forms the initial special jurisdiction, and the voluntary acquisition sequence.
  • Legal entity formation - the vehicle to negotiate, hold capital, and sign agreements does not yet exist.
